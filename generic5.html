Volleyball Serving Drills

To serve the volleyball you should ready your proper stance.You want to stand light on your feet with little further slightly more than shoulder-width apart. Walk or move unsteadily as if about to fall in an up-and-back position. Make sure your feet are planted on the ground and you are not standing on your toes. You will begin with your weight shifted to your back foot, while maintaining your stance with your front foot fully on the ground as well.

Pick up the ball. You should hold the ball in your secondary hand, with your other hand put down gently or carefully at your side. Hold the ball in front of your body, above your hips and just below your waist.Don’t hold the ball too far out from your upper body, otherwise you won’t be able to hit it with your opposite hand.on’t grip the ball too firmly, but rather let it rest on your palm using a gentle grip with your fingers to keep it from falling.

Check your position of yur body. Your upper body and shoulders should be leaning forward slightly, and you should keep your eyes on the ball at all times. Close your hand, with your fingers tucked and tidy in and your thumb resting at the side. Using your hand with a closed fist, swing your arm in a pendulum motion to hit the ball. Aim or think to hit the ball just below the center, so as to drive or push something forward slightly upwards and over the net.
